    How to make a successful Masters application:
    To maximise your chances of gaining a place on a Masters course at the University you should:

  • Link the subject to your own goals , clearly communicate the relationship of the subject to your own research, professional training needs, goals and future plans.
  • You will need to Indicate your motivation ,reflect on your intellectual and practical motivation for undertaking the course and why you find the subject area especially interesting or challenging.
  • You will need to Build on your prior knowledge and be ready to explain how the Masters course will build on the knowledge you have already built up in the subject area.
  • You will need to prove your commitment and show understanding of the chosen subject along with sensible preparation for taking the course.
  • However you will need to think about the course structure and make you link clearly to the description of the course content and perhaps provide evidence of consultation with the prospective course leader.
  • Finally show your interest and point out if there are any specific areas of the course that are of interest to you, such as particular modules, placement opportunities or practical experience.

  • Make sure you understand your thesis topic and be able to indicate clear aims and objectives, key research questions to be answered and the research methods to be utilised.
